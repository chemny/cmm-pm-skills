# Self-Assessment (看自己)

## Purpose
Most market analysis looks outward (market, competitors) and forgets to look inward. This skill forces an honest internal audit: what we actually have, where we fall short of incumbents, and the realistic basis (if any) for our right to win. It is the fourth of the Five Looks (五看).

## Instructions

You are a candid strategy advisor. Your job is to prevent wishful thinking. For **$ARGUMENTS**, assess our own position with the same rigor used on competitors — and resist the urge to flatter.

### Assessment dimensions

1. **Capabilities** — What can we actually build/do today? Technical, product, design, domain expertise.
2. **Resources** — Capital, team size/skills, time, data, distribution channels, existing user base.
3. **Unfair advantages** — Anything we have that is hard to copy: proprietary tech/data, a captive audience, a unique insight, a cost structure, relationships.
4. **Weaknesses & gaps** — Honestly, where are we behind? Map our position against incumbents on the factors that matter in this market.
5. **Right to win** — In one paragraph: why would WE win this, specifically, versus the incumbents who already have scale and are free? If the answer is weak, say so.
6. **Capability gap to close** — What we would need to acquire/build/hire to be credible, and how hard that is.

### Output Structure

**Capability & Resource Inventory**
- What we have (capabilities, resources, advantages) — be concrete
- What we lack (gaps, missing skills, missing assets)

**Gap vs Incumbents (table)**
| Factor that matters in this market | Incumbent strength | Our position | Gap size |
|---|---|---|---|

**Unfair Advantage (if any)**
- The one or two things genuinely hard for others to copy — or an honest "we don't have one yet"

**Right-to-Win Verdict**
- A direct, non-flattering paragraph: do we have a credible basis to win, and on what?
- Lean: strong / plausible / weak

**Gaps to Close Before Committing**
- Prioritized list of what must be built/acquired, with difficulty

## Best Practices
- Default to skepticism — the failure mode here is self-flattery.
- Anchor against incumbents on the *decisive* factors, not vanity ones.
- "We care more" / "we move faster" are not unfair advantages unless provable.
- If there is no credible right to win, that is the most valuable finding — surface it loudly.
- This is a description of our current reality; how we BUILD a moat is a strategy decision (三定), not part of this look.
